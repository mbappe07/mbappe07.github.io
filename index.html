A salty problem

In context of global warming and changes in hydrological regimes, governments and private companies will need to maintain public safety and contain environmental and socio-economic impacts of extreme natural events. Extreme winter conditions are no exception, with an expected increase in frequency and intensity of snowfall events. Facing this situation, maintenance of public safety must be ensured through winter maintenance operations, where de-icing agents are largely achieved through the use of salts. As a matter of fact, Canadian collectivities would have to increase road salts application as de-icing agents are required for road safety. Unfortunately, the often-excessive application of road salts since the 1940s has led to major environmental problems. Since road salts are widely applied throughout Canadian roads, the growing issue of chloride contaminated water is turning into one of the current pressing environmental issues. Approximately 7,700 lakes of North America would face elevated chloride concentrations due to road salt runoff [1]. Salts accumulation in water bodies affects both aquatic ecosystems and the surrounding fauna and flora where salinity can even reach lethal levels for some species.

The real cost of salt use

These environmental impacts have a major economic impact, with an estimated $1.9 billion in annual environmental damage costs in Canada [2]. In addition, corrosive effect of road salts generates indirect costs related to damage in public infrastructure and vehicles at nearly $9.4 billion annually in Canada [2].

Our solution, guiA

The growing issue of chloride contaminated water is one of the current pressing environmental issues related to urban watersheds, that represents a threat for Canada’s freshwater ecosystems. Because the application of de-icing agents in urban areas remains necessary during the winter, Clean Nature is convinced that the most feasible and long-term solution to divert salt from entering the aquatic ecosystems is only through source reduction. To achieve this goal, it is necessary to provide the right tools to those involved in winter maintenance, so that they can optimize the road salts use while minimizing environmental impacts. Hence, Clean Nature focus on preventing excessive salt roads use through guiA, an adapted artificial intelligence model based on local real-time meteorological, environmental and road conditions as well as specific properties of different de-icing products.
